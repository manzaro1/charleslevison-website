About the Artist

Painter. Teacher. Filmmaker. Storyteller.

Artist Portrait / Studio Shot

My Story

I was born and raised in Mangochi, a town along the shores of Lake Malawi. Growing up, the lake was my playground and my muse. I spent countless hours watching fishermen cast their nets at dawn, the way light danced on the water, and how the landscape transformed with each season.

My formal art education began at [Institution Name], where I developed my oil painting technique. But my true education came from observing life in Malawian markets, villages, and along the lake's shores.

Today, at 31, I split my time between my painting practice, teaching art to young Malawians, and filmmaking. Each discipline informs the other—my films capture movement and narrative, while my paintings freeze moments of extraordinary beauty in everyday life.

My work is deeply personal yet universally resonant. I paint what I know—the warm colors of jacaranda trees in bloom, the silhouettes of fishermen against the setting sun, the resilience of communities that face life's challenges with grace.

"

Art is memory made visible. Each painting is an act of preservation—capturing not just what a place looks like, but what it feels like to stand there. When I paint the lake at sunrise, I'm not just documenting a view; I'm sharing the cool morning air, the sound of lapping waves, the hope of a new day.

My impressionistic style allows me to explore light and color in ways that feel true to the Malawian landscape. We have some of the most dramatic skies in the world—storm clouds gathering over the lake, golden hour turning everything amber, sudden downpours that transform the streets into mirrors.

I paint because I must. Because there's beauty in our everyday moments that deserves to be celebrated, preserved, and shared with the world.

— Charles Levison
